Wallenpaupack Area School District

Mission Statement

Provide an educational environment that promotes excellence for all students, thereby developing life-long learners who are responsible citizens.

What Does This Mean?

This means:

  • All students have a career/education plan
  • All students are problem solvers
  • All students have a deep understanding of and experiences in citizenship
  • students understand mathematical concepts and processes at or above grade level
  • All students reading and writing at or above grade level

By 2021, every Wallenpaupack student will graduate with a strong understanding of careers, be thoroughly engaged in learning, and make smart choices about education, training and careers beyond high school. To accomplish this, we are developing a K-12 approach to career awareness, exploration and immersion, and by 8th grade, all students will develop a personalized Career and Education Plan that guides their career exploration experiences during high school and shapes their post-high school goals for education, training, and careers. We are beginning to organize our high school into themed academies, with each offering one or more College-Career Pathway Programs. Students will enroll in a Pathway Program of study to guide their high school preparation for college and careers.

Career Academy Plan

Freshman Academy

  • Freshman Seminar Course
    • Focused alignment of individual strengths and interests to broad career fields
    • Development of the 10 for 10 Career and Education Plan (10 year plan beginning in 10th grade)
  • Apply for enrollment in a Career Academy beginning in 10th grade
  • Schedule Career Pathway program of study
  • Develop procedures for using multiple data sources, in real time, to intervene, support or expand studentsâ€™ capacity to learn, i.e. data teams.

Sophmores, Juniors & Seniors

  • Begin a Career Pathway in a Career Academy
  • Students select pathway in the scheduling process
  • Development of Academy Ambassador program
    • Extensively trained students become Ambassadors
  • Complete Experiential Learning 10th and 11th grade milestones
    • Work-site tours, job-shadows, college tour, etc
  • 12th Grade Capstone Course
    • Culminating seminar which includes personal finance, completion of Electronic Career Portfolio and graduation presentation
  • Graduate with a Career Transition Plan

STEM Integration

STEM incorporates knowledge of science, technology, engineering, and math to develop an understanding of how the world works and how to solve problems. Coupling STEM with the creativity nurtured in the arts and design, it is explicitly part of our science, technology, engineering, mathematics, fine and practical arts, theater, and music courses. It is a driving principle of curriculum, program and activity design for all WAHS courses. Our goal is for all WAHS students to use STEM and Arts experiences to become problem solvers in their future careers and personal lives.
